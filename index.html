Hi!

The Academy for Science and Design (ASD) is a Blue-ribbon public charter school located in Nashua, New Hampshire, United States. ASD concentrates on providing a STEM-based education. The academy is tuition-free for New Hampshire residents. However, the limited space available for new students makes a lottery system necessary. All applicants who are eligible for the lottery (who completed the application requirements by the deadline) are drawn and this order is used to create the waiting list each year. The school is physically located in Nashua, but is a New Hampshire public school and all New Hampshire students are eligible to apply for enrollment. Nashua students are not given priority over students from any other New Hampshire towns. ASD opened in September 2007 and is currently serving 525 students from over 40 New Hampshire communities in grades 6 through 12. The academy offers a science, technology, engineering and math oriented curriculum beginning with algebra, integrated biology and chemistry, and physics in grade 7.

Awards

Best high school

In 2015, the Academy for Science and Design was ranked the 50th best high school in America by Newsweek., and rose to 44th in 2016.[12] It is currently ranked as the best high school in New Hampshire. On September 28, 2017, ten years after opening for their first year, the school won the National Blue Ribbon Schools award.

What's New

  • Flipped Classroom

    In the flipped classroom, the instruction and “homework” are, well, flipped. The content is delivered at home through short videos, reading assignments, animated power points, and a variety of other methods. Class time then becomes much more interactive and individualized, all with the help and guidance of the teacher. Rather than work on the “hard stuff” while at home, isolated, they complete their work in the presence of their teacher, allowing them to get answers, advice, and support immediately.

    Video lectures/demonstrations will vary in length depending on the objectives of the lesson. While there is not a set length for the videos, instructors will be mindful of screen-time and focus their video modules to key concepts and understanding.

    Depending on the class and the teacher, there are times when the learning videos will be shortened in length because instructors will need to assign other reading, writing, and project work to be done outside of the Zoom class period.
